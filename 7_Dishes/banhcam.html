    Banh Cam is the latest Vietnamese dish to be featured by American media as one of the top must-try delicacies for food enthusiasts.

    By Như Quỳnh
    Published October 17, 2024

    Banh cam, banh cong are often found piled high on trays, coated in a shiny layer of sugar and sprinkled with white sesame seeds. These snacks evoke childhood memories for many.

    Banh cam, named for its round shape resembling an orange, is called a fried cake by those from the north, while banh cong, shaped like a donut, gets its name from the circular form known as ‘còng’ in the Mekong Delta. Frequently sold together, their ingredients are quite similar; the main difference is that banh cam is filled with mung bean paste, whereas banh cong is not.

    The dough is made from glutinous rice flour, and some regions add yellow sweet potato for flavor. The filling consists of mashed mung beans mixed with sugar, shaped into balls. After kneading, the dough is rolled out, filled, and wrapped. For banh cong, the dough is shaped into long strips and formed into rings. Once shaped, the cakes are rolled in sesame seeds and deep-fried until golden.

    After frying, the cakes are cooled and drizzled with a light syrup that enhances their orange color. Some banh cam are dusted with white sugar for those who prefer less sweetness. A good cake should have a crispy exterior, with the sugar coating not too chewy. The filling and dough remain distinct, allowing diners to enjoy the crunchy shell, followed by the creamy, fragrant mung bean filling and the enticing aroma of the fried dough.
