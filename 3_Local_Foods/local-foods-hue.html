    Local Food

    The Exquisite Street Food of Hue

    Hue

    Banh Canh Nam Pho: A Savory Hue Noodle Soup

    Banh Canh Nam Pho is a specialty noodle soup from Hue, characterized by its thick, chewy noodles and savory broth made from crab and pork. The dish is often garnished with fresh herbs and served hot, offering a unique and hearty dining experience.

    Hue

    Banh Bot Loc: A Delicate Hue Dumpling

    Banh Bot Loc is a traditional Hue dish featuring translucent tapioca dumplings filled with shrimp and pork. It is typically wrapped in banana leaves and steamed, offering a chewy texture and a savory filling.

    Hue

    Banh Nam: A Hue-Style Flat Rice Dumpling

    Banh Nam is a traditional dish made from flat rice flour wrapped around a filling of shrimp and pork, then steamed in banana leaves. Its soft texture and delicate flavor make it a favorite among Hue locals.

    Hue

    Banh Xeo: A Crispy Vietnamese Pancake

    Banh Xeo is a Vietnamese pancake made from rice flour, turmeric, and coconut milk. In Hue, it is filled with shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts, and served with fresh herbs and dipping sauce.

    Hue

    Bun Bo Hue: Spicy Beef Noodle Soup

    Bun Bo Hue is a famous spicy beef noodle soup from Hue, featuring rice vermicelli in a rich, spicy broth made from lemongrass and chili. The dish is typically garnished with herbs, lime, and chili oil.

    Hue

    Che Hue: Traditional Vietnamese Sweet Soup

    Che Hue is a popular dessert in Hue, consisting of various sweet soups made from beans, fruits, and coconut milk. It offers a delightful mix of textures and can be served hot or cold.

    Hue

    Com Hen: A Unique Rice Dish with Clams

    Com Hen is a specialty of Hue, consisting of steamed rice topped with tiny boiled clams, herbs, peanuts, and crispy pork skin. The dish has a distinct flavor, combining the sweetness of clams and the richness of herbs.

    Hue

    Nem Lui Hue: Grilled Lemongrass Skewers

    Nem Lui Hue features grilled pork skewers marinated with lemongrass. It is typically served with rice paper, fresh herbs, and a dipping sauce, creating a flavorful and refreshing dish.

    Hue

    Tom Chua Hue: Hue's Unique Pickled Shrimp

    Tom Chua Hue is a special type of pickled shrimp from Hue, prepared with chili, garlic, and rice vinegar. It has a tangy, spicy flavor that pairs well with steamed rice or other dishes.

    Hue

    Tre Hue: A Fermented Pork Dish

    Tre Hue is a traditional dish made from fermented pork, wrapped in banana leaves. It has a slightly tangy flavor and is often enjoyed as a snack or appetizer.
