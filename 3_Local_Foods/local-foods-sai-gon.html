    The Exquisite Street Food of Sai Gon

    Sai Gon

    Com Tam: Broken Rice Delight

    Com Tam is a beloved Vietnamese dish made from broken rice. Typically served with grilled pork (often marinated), it can also include fried eggs, pork skin, and various pickled vegetables. Accompanied by a side of fish sauce for dipping, Com Tam offers a delicious combination of flavors and textures. This hearty meal is popular in Ho Chi Minh City and is often enjoyed as a comforting street food option.

    Sai Gon

    Banh Trang Tron: Vietnamese Rice Paper Salad

    Banh Trang Tron is a popular Vietnamese street food dish that features a mix of rice paper (banh trang) cut into strips and combined with various fresh ingredients. Typically, it includes shredded vegetables, herbs, and proteins like shrimp or pork.

    Sai Gon

    Bap Xao: Vietnamese Stir-Fried Corn

    Bap Xao is a popular Vietnamese street food dish made from stir-fried corn. The sweet corn is often mixed with ingredients like butter, onions, and sometimes added proteins such as shrimp or chicken. Seasoned with spices and garnished with fresh herbs, Bap Xao offers a deliciously savory and slightly sweet flavor. This quick and tasty snack is a favorite among locals, especially enjoyed during outdoor gatherings and markets.

    Sai Gon

    Bo Ne: Vietnamese Sizzling Beef

    Bo Ne is a popular Vietnamese dish featuring sizzling beef served on a hot plate. The dish typically includes marinated beef, often accompanied by fried eggs, pâté, and fresh vegetables. Served with a side of crusty bread, Bo Ne offers a delightful mix of flavors and textures, making it a hearty meal. It's commonly enjoyed for breakfast or lunch and is a favorite in many Vietnamese eateries. The sizzling sound and aroma add to the overall dining experience.

    Sai Gon

    Bun Mam: Fermented Fish Noodle Soup

    Bun Mam is a flavorful Vietnamese noodle soup made from fermented fish broth. It features rice noodles (bun) topped with a variety of ingredients, such as shrimp, squid, and pork, along with fresh herbs and vegetables. The dish is known for its rich, pungent flavor, often enhanced with chili and lime. Bun Mam is particularly popular in southern Vietnam, offering a unique and aromatic dining experience that showcases the region's culinary heritage.

    Sai Gon

    Hu Tieu Nam Vang: Cambodian-Influenced Noodle Soup

    Hu Tieu Nam Vang is a beloved Vietnamese noodle soup that has Cambodian influences. It features a mix of rice noodles (hu tieu) served in a savory broth, often made from pork and seafood. Typically garnished with a variety of toppings, such as sliced pork, shrimp, and fresh herbs, this dish is known for its rich flavors and aromatic broth. Hu Tieu Nam Vang is commonly enjoyed for breakfast or lunch, making it a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

    Sai Gon

    Lau De: Vietnamese Goat Hot Pot

    Lau De is a popular Vietnamese goat hot pot dish known for its rich and aromatic flavors. The hot pot features tender goat meat, cooked with a variety of vegetables, herbs, and spices in a flavorful broth. Typically served with rice noodles or steamed rice, Lau De allows diners to cook their ingredients at the table, creating a communal dining experience. This dish is especially enjoyed during gatherings and is appreciated for its unique taste and hearty nature.

    Sai Gon

    Sup Cua: Vietnamese Crab Soup

    Sup Cua is a delicious Vietnamese crab soup known for its rich and flavorful broth. Made with fresh crab meat, the soup often includes ingredients like quail eggs, mushrooms, and various herbs. Thickened with cornstarch, Sup Cua has a smooth texture and is typically garnished with green onions and cilantro. This comforting dish is often enjoyed as an appetizer or as part of a meal, offering a delightful seafood flavor that is beloved in Vietnamese cuisine.
